Tatum App Store Copy - Minimal Accuracy Edits

This version keeps Alanna's wording as intact as possible. The only goal is to remove or soften statements that could conflict with the app's actual account/name/email setup.

Editorial rule: do not explain backend plumbing in the store description. Only remove claims that are too absolute.

Opening Privacy Claim

Removed only the unsafe phrase
Initial wording

Tatum is a private wellness app built for women who want to understand their own intimate lives on their terms, in their own words, completely off the grid. No accounts. No cloud. No one else ever sees your data.

Everything you log lives only on your device, protected by Face ID, Touch ID, or your personal passcode.

Revised wording

Tatum is a private wellness app built for women who want to understand their own intimate lives on their terms, in their own words.

Everything you log lives only on your device, protected by Face ID, Touch ID, or your personal passcode where available.

Why: This keeps the original shape and simply removes the claims that are too broad because the app does have account/sign-in behavior.

Private Journal

One phrase softened
Initial wording

Write notes on any session or an honest answer to why you did not want to engage or initiate. Over time your journal reveals patterns your mind cannot see alone. Completely private and encrypted on your device.

Revised wording

Write notes on any session or an honest answer to why you did not want to engage or initiate. Over time your journal reveals patterns your mind cannot see alone. Stored locally on your device.

Why: "Stored locally on your device" is accurate and much lower-risk than claiming a specific encryption implementation in marketing copy.

Zero Data Policy

Changed because "zero data" is too broad
Initial wording

ZERO DATA POLICY

Tatum never collects your data. No server ever receives what you log. Delete the app and everything disappears with no traces left behind.

Revised wording

WHAT YOU LOG STAYS YOURS

Tatum never collects what you log. No server ever receives your entries. Delete your local data and what you logged disappears from your device.

Why: We kept the core point about logs staying local, but removed "never collects your data" and "delete everything" because those could conflict with account and purchase records.

Privacy Bullets

Kept short, removed false absolutes
Initial wording
  • Stored only on your phone
  • Face ID / Touch ID / Passcode protected
  • Delete the app, delete everything
  • Your name stays on your device only and is never transmitted
Revised wording
  • Entries stored only on your phone
  • Face ID / Touch ID / Passcode protected where available
  • Delete your local data whenever you choose

Why: This avoids the name/transmission claim and keeps the focus on what the user logs rather than all possible account data.
